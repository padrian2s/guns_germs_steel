Summary

Population replacement patterns: Bantu languages replaced African Pygmy/Khoisan indigenous languages in subequatorial Africa. East Asia question: what enabled Sino-Tibetan speakers spread from North China to South China? What enabled Austroasiatic speakers expand into tropical Southeast Asia? Population replacement requires technological/political/agricultural advantages. Language substitution indicates successful population competitionâ€”incoming populations expanded territory replacing indigenous languages. Archaeological evidence determines what advantages enabled replacement.

Key Concepts

Population replacement requires technological advantage. Agricultural superiority determines expansion capacity. Political organization enables conquest. Bantu iron tools superior to Pygmy/Khoisan stone tools enabling expansion. Sino-Tibetan agriculture superior to South China native populations enabling expansion. Agricultural technology determines military capacity determining conquest success. Technological advantage determines population replacement outcomes.

Historical & Geographic Context

Africa: Bantu iron-working pastoralists (1,000-2,000 years ago) expanded south/east replacing Pygmy/Khoisan hunter-gatherers. Technological advantage (iron tools, cattle pastoralism) enabled expansion. Asia: Sino-Tibetan millet/rice agriculturalists (3,000-4,000 years ago) expanded south replacing South China hunter-gatherer/primitive agriculturalist populations. Austroasiatic spread southward into tropical Southeast Asia replacing indigenous populations. Agricultural technology driving force behind all population replacements.
