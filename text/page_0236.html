Summary

Linear A/B scripts: restricted palace/administrative use. Greek alphabet: immediately used for poetry/humor in private homes. First Greek alphabetic example (~740 BCE) Athenian wine jug poetry announcing dancing contest. Alphabet enabled literary use impossible with earlier restricted scripts. Alphabet simplicity enabling widespread adoption beyond administrative class.

Key Concepts

Writing system sophistication determines usage scope. Alphabet simplicity enables rapid literacy spread. Complex scripts restricted to specialized scribes. Simple alphabet accessible to broader population enabling literary use. Writing system evolution determines cultural development potential.

Historical & Geographic Context

Linear B Crete: administrative records only (palace accounting). Greek alphabet adoption ~800 BCE Mediterranean regions. Immediate literary use demonstrates alphabet advantages. Widespread private literacy unknown before alphabet. Alphabet democratized writing enabling poetry, humor, private communication.
