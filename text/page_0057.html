Summary

Moriori and Maori diverged completely despite common ancestry and contact-free separation. Australian seal ship brought knowledge of Chathams to New Zealand, triggering 1835 invasion. Message advertised Chathams' resources and unwarlike inhabitantsâ€”this geographic knowledge gap caused invasion. Centuries of isolation created distinct societies: one peaceful hunter-gatherers, one warlike farmers.

Key Concepts

Divergent evolutionary paths from same ancestor. Geographic isolation produced: opposite subsistence modes, opposite warfare traditions, opposite technology complexity. Knowledge gap from isolation triggered collision. Demonstrates environmental determination of societal type.

Historical & Geographic Context

Moriori separated from Maori ~500+ years. No contact until 1835. Each developed independently: Moriori toward simplification, Maori toward complexity. Geographic isolation enabled divergence; knowledge deficit enabled invasion.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geography shaped opposite outcomes from same population. Shows environmental determination operates quickly (centuries) and completely. Larger point: continental-scale geographic differences would produce even larger divergence over 13,000 years. Polynesia tests model before applying to continents.
