Geographic advantages: enormous head start in human history, highly diverse climates/habitats, highest human diversity globally. 10,000 years ago observer might predict Europe becoming sub-Saharan African empire vassal states. Key Concepts

Initial geographic advantage insufficient sustained advantage. Africa head start thousands of years: domestication emerged Africa first. Eurasia subsequently developed advantages (transcontinental diffusion, connected domesticates). Africa geographic fragmentation (deserts, forests, equatorial barriers) prevented technology diffusion prevented competitive advantage. Initial advantage eroded through subsequent geography-dependent limitations.

Historical & Geographic Context

Africa 10,000 years ago: agriculture emerging, civilization developing (Egypt), technology accumulation beginning. Eurasia simultaneously: agriculture emerging, diffusion advantages (connected populations, east-west axes). Subsequent 8000 years: Eurasia geography enabled technology diffusion enabled civilizations competition enabled military technology development. Africa geography fragmentation prevented equivalent diffusion prevented equivalent technology accumulation prevented equivalent civilization competition.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic advantages compound differentially over time. Africa initial advantage (human origin) insufficient overcoming subsequent geographic disadvantages (fragmentation). Eurasia subsequent advantages (connected domesticates, east-west axis diffusion) compound accelerating. Advantage inequality geography-rooted: Africa fragmentation limiting diffusion limiting competition limiting innovation. Reversing initial advantage prediction (Africa dominating Europe) demonstrates geography determines long-term advantage. Initial advantages insufficient if subsequent geography creates disadvantages.
