Summary

Agricultural diffusion failures illustrate geographic constraints on spread. California farming failed to spread from U.S. Southwest (climate/ecology barriers). Australia failed to receive farming from New Guinea/Indonesia (ocean barriers + climate mismatch). South Africa Cape failed to receive farming from Natal Province (climate barriers). Spread rates varied dramatically across regions. East-west spread rapid; north-south spread slow/failed.

Key Concepts

Geographic barriers prevent agricultural diffusion. Climate-compatible zones enable spread; climate-mismatched zones prevent spread. Ocean barriers prevent diffusion (Australia isolation). North-south climate gradients prevent technology transfer (California/Southwest). East-west similar-latitude zones enable rapid spread. Geographic proximity insufficient without climate/latitude compatibility.

Historical & Geographic Context

California Mediterranean climate differs from U.S. Southwest arid climate. Australia tropical not compatible with New Guinea montane. Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic latitude/climate determines diffusion success. East-west axes enable diffusion (same latitude/season/climate). North-south axes prevent diffusion (different climate/growing season). Barriers (ocean, climate zones) absolute. Civilizations separated by incompatible climate zones cannot achieve agricultural technology transfer. Geography determines which regions receive technology diffusion versus remain isolated.
