Rezumat

Five major African human groups by A.D. 1000: blacks (formerly Africa-confined), whites (Afroasiatic origin), Pygmies (African-endemic), Khoisan (African-endemic), Asians (recent arrivals). Geographic distributions reflect population movements and original homelands. Racial categories obscure geographic population history. Modern distributions result from historical geographic expansions and contractions.

Concepte Cheie

Population geography reflects historical expansion/contraction. Blacks: Bantu agricultural expansion determined sub-Saharan distribution. Whites: Mediterranean origin, limited to coastal regions originally. Pygmies: rainforest-endemic, displaced by Bantu. Khoisan: hunter-gatherers, displaced to margins by Bantu. Asians: trade-driven arrivals. Population geographic distribution indicates historical advantage/disadvantage. Current distributions reflect geographic-determined population success rates.
