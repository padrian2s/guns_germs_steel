Key Concepts

Population replacement geography-determined creates language engulfing. Dominant population languages spread suppressing earlier languages. Language fragmentation indicates historical expansion by larger population. Geographic marginalization forces populations to harsh environments. Language survival geographic-limited: marginal zones sustain displaced languages. Click consonant language unique feature reflects linguistic isolation (margin populations limited language contact).

Historical & Geographic Context

Nilo-Saharan: once widespread sub-Saharan and Saharan regions, today fragmented patches. Bantu and Afroasiatic expansion pushed speakers into isolated pockets. Khoisan (San Bushmen): dominated southern Africa before Bantu expansion (~2000 years ago). Bantu agricultural advantage displaced San into Kalahari and coastal margins (least desirable land). !Kung and other Khoisan groups: only surviving in deserts, coasts unsuitable for agriculture.
