Summary

Diamond summarizes millions of years of human evolution from ape ancestors (~7 million years ago) to modern humans. Great apes' African origin indicates human evolution also occurred in Africa. Early hominins (Australopithecus, Homo habilis, Homo erectus) developed upright posture, larger brains, and stone tools, but remained confined to Africa for millions of years.

Key Concepts

Early human evolution was African phenomenon. Homo erectus first spread beyond Africa to Java ~1 million years ago. By 500,000 years ago, Homo sapiens appeared in Africa and Europe. Early stone tools were crude; Australia, Americas remained unpeopled (required boat technology or Siberian occupation).

Historical & Geographic Context

Human evolution paralleled African geography for millions of years. Geographic barriers (water, climate) prevented human spread to Australia and Americas until much later. Africa's role as humanity's cradle established it as center of early technological development.

Connection to Main Thesis

Africa's early human evolution advantage did not translate to lasting technological superiority. This early head start became irrelevant by 11,000 BCE, proving geography's changing effect on civilizational advantage depending on specific environmental conditions (domesticable species, climate, etc.).
