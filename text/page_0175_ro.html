Rezumat

Three reasons Eurasia dominated domesticable animals: First, Eurasia/Africa larger land area with greater megafauna diversity, started with most candidates. Second, Australia/Americas suffered late-Pleistocene extinctions eliminating candidates. Mammals exposed to humans suddenly when hunting skills peak. Eurasia/Africa mammoths survived later. Third, higher percentage Eurasian survivors proved domesticable.

Concepte Cheie

Extinction timing determines survival. Late exposure to skilled hunters: megafauna vulnerable. Early/gradual human expansion: coexistence enabling domestication. Geographic size determines megafauna diversity. Extinction wave eliminated Americas/Australia candidates. Eurasian survival preserved domesticable species. Geographic extinction patterns determined animal availability.
