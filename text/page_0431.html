European expansion leveraged geographic advantages accumulated over millennia: Old World disease burden immunized populations, domesticated animals provided military advantage, agricultural crops ensured nutrition. Americas lacked parallel disease/animal advantages geography didn't provide domestication possibilities previously.

Key Concepts

Columbian Exchange: bidirectional biological transfer across Atlantic. Americas gained Old World crops (wheat, barley, sugarcane), animals (horses, cattle, pigs), diseases (smallpox, measles, plague). Old World gained American crops (maize, potatoes, tobacco) but recovered disadvantage through disease advantage. Geographic isolation produced separate evolutionary trajectories. Americas megafauna extinction eliminated large domestication sources. Ecological Imperialism concept: European biology expanded into Americas filled ecological niches. Disease immunodeficiency: isolated American populations had no epidemic experience, vulnerable Old World pathogens. Domestication advantage: Old World horses, cattle provided technology leverage.

Historical & Geographic Context

Columbian Exchange documented botanically and zoologically: maize/potatoes/tobacco from Americas transformed Old World agriculture and society; wheat/barley/horses from Old World transformed Americas. 1492 meeting point of 12,000-year geographic isolation. Americas: no domesticated large quadrupeds (llamas/alpacas insufficient), no epidemic diseases (small isolated populations didn't generate plague/smallpox), limited food crops suitable for Eurasia. Old World: domesticated most large animals, accumulated plague/smallpox/measles through millennial urbanization and domestication, adapted crops across latitude. 1492 Spanish conquest demonstrating disease and animal advantage cascade from geographic domestication differences.

Connection to Main Thesis

Columbian Exchange proves geographic determinism thesis: biology itself geography-distributed. Disease burden, domesticated animals, crop types geography-determined through separate continental evolution. Old World advantages (disease immunity, domesticated horses, trained metallurgy enabling steel) accumulated through geography providing domesticable species. Spanish conquest mechanism: diseases did killing (small pox eliminated 95% American populations before major fighting). Horses provided mobile combat advantage. Steel weapons leveraged metallurgical advantage. All derived from geographic domestication availability. Americas' lack of domesticable megafauna meant no horses, no plow agriculture, no epidemic diseases building immunity. Biological comparison proves geography determines even microbial history determining geopolitical outcomes.
