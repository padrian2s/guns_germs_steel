Summary

Eurasian empires dominated. Americas had sole two empires: Aztec and Inca. Resembled Eurasian counterparts: large size, populations, polyglot composition, official religions, conquest origins. However: only two empires in Americas. Eurasian empires: multiple (Roman, Persian, Chinese, Mongol, Ottoman). Difference reflects geographic capacity for civilization complexity.

Key Concepts

Empire rarity in Americas. Geography limited civilization complexity. Domesticable animals scarcity limited military power. Diseases fewer in Americas limited epidemic effects on population control. Writing (Mayan) didn't diffuse widely enabling empire administration. Eurasian animal domestication enabled cavalry, military technology advantage.

Historical & Geographic Context

Aztec Empire: lacked large domestic animals, used hand weapons, relied on numerical advantage. Inca Empire: lacked horses and pigs limiting cavalry warfare. Eurasian empires: cavalry-dominant, iron tools, epidemic diseases reducing conquered population's resistance. Conquistador handful defeated empires lacking equivalent military technology.
