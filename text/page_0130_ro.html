Rezumat

Artificial selection through human harvesting. Supermarket strawberries vastly larger than wild ancestors. Differences arose from natural genetic variation plus selective harvesting. Some morphological changes (large fruit) advantageous for farmers. Others (wild berry colors) required conscious protection (nets, greenhouses) against predators.

Concepte Cheie

Artificial selection pressure. Cultivated plants selected for: larger seeds/fruits, reduced toxins, synchronized ripening, reduced dispersal. Natural genetic variation in wild populations provides selection material. Some traits selected for unconsciously (collecting larger seeds), others required conscious intervention (pest protection).
