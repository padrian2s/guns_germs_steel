Key Concepts

Clovis horizon: 11,000-year-old widespread culture marking first evidence human settlement Americas. Pre-Clovis sites: controversial earlier evidence suggesting arrival 15,000+ years ago. Radiocarbon dating: archaeological dating method measuring carbon-14 decay determining artifact age. Geochronology: correlation between climate change and human cultural development. Climate change timing corresponds agriculture emergence: warming post-glacial period created plant growth enabling domestication. Geographic availability domesticable species (maize, beans, squash in Mesoamerica; potatoes in Andes) limits when agriculture could develop. Settlement permanent only when agriculture sustainable. Settlement timeline constrained by geography: geography determines when glacial-interglacial cycles enabled/prevented crossing.
