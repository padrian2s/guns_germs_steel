Rezumat

Diamond extends the question from 1500 CE back to 11,000 BCE. By the end of the Ice Age, all humans were hunter-gatherers. Between then and 1500 CE, some continents developed agriculture, metallurgy, and writing while others remained hunter-gatherers. This differential development rate is history's fundamental pattern and the book's core subject.

Concepte Cheie

Diamond identifies "history's broadest pattern": the different development rates of continents from 11,000 BCE to 1500 CE. Eurasia achieved all major technologies thousands of years before other continents. The pattern indicates geography, not culture or biology, must have channeled different advantages to different regions.
