Rezumat

Tribes: intermediate political organization between bands and chiefdoms. Maintain alliances with neighboring hamlets regardless of language differences (example: Fore-speakers and different language groups maintaining ties). Tribes occupy much of modern New Guinea, Melanesia, Amazonia. Archaeological evidence: substantial settlements in past lacked chiefdom archaeological hallmarks. Tribal organization inferred from settlement size without state-level institutions. Tribes represent larger organized groups than bands but without hereditary leadership.

Concepte Cheie

Tribe: population scale 100s-1000s enabling cooperation beyond kinship. Tribal alliances maintain peace across language/ethnic boundaries. Tribal organization requires larger resources than bands but less surplus than chiefdoms. Archaeological signatures: settlement size without central authority indicators (fortifications, elite burials, ceremonial centers). Geographic conditions permitting moderate population concentration without full agricultural transition. Tribe size reflects geographic carrying capacity larger than bands but unable to support state institutions.
