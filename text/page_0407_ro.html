Rezumat

Societal advantage acquisition mechanisms: societies without advantages either acquire them from advantage-bearing societies or are replaced. Maori New Zealand Musket Wars example: societies initially lacking muskets obtained them, preventing replacement. Technology diffusion + migration create equilibration mechanisms. Second causal set: diffusion and migration rates determining advantage spread speed continent-specific. Eurasian highest diffusion rates.

Concepte Cheie

Technology diffusion rate continent-specific through geography. Geographic connectivity enables rapid diffusion. Geographic isolation limits diffusion. Migration enables technology spread and population replacement. Societies with advantages expand; societies without adopt advantages or replaced. Technology advantage temporary if diffusion occurs. Geographic factors determine diffusion rate determine advantage persistence rate.
