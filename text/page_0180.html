Summary

Crop spread preempts related domestication. Southwest Asia: rapid spread makes domestication of alternate species pointless. Already-grown peas eliminate motivation to domesticate wild pea ancestors or related wild pea species. Crop diffusion faster than independent domestication. Geographic diffusion determines which crops domesticated vs. diffused.

Key Concepts

Diffusion vs. independent domestication. Rapid diffusion preempts local domestication. Economic rationality: why domesticate inferior crop if superior diffused crop available? Geographic diffusion timing determines adoption pattern. Regions receiving crops via diffusion skip domestication. Isolation enables independent domestication.

Historical & Geographic Context

Southwest Asia crops: spread rapidly to Europe, Africa. Recipient populations adopted rather than independently domesticated. Peas: farmers received crops rather than domesticate wild ancestors. Mesoamerica: isolated development independent domestication (maize, beans, squash). Americas: received Old World crops post-1500 CE, adopted rather than domesticate.
