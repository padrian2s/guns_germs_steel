Key Concepts

Oceanic migration technology determines population distribution. Southeast Asians possessed seagoing technology (outrigger canoes) enabling long-distance oceanic crossing. African populations (pre-oceanic navigation) confined land routes. Technology determines possible migration routes determines population dispersal. Historical & Geographic Context

Southeast Asian outrigger canoe technology: seagoing navigation Indian Ocean trade routes (Arab/Indian traders). Madagascar settlement ~400-800 AD from Southeast Asia. Bantu populations reached Madagascar later from Africa (after Austronesian arrival). Madagascar today: mixed Austronesian (lighter skin, Asian features) and Bantu (darker skin, African features) ancestry. Languages: Malagasy (Austronesian language) official despite African location. Population mixture documents two separate migration waves from opposite continents.

Connection to Main Thesis

Population geography determined by technology enabling migration. Southeast Asian oceanic technology enabled crossing Indian Ocean (Africa-unreachable without equivalent technology). African Bantu lacked seagoing technology limited land migration. Technology advantage determines possible settlements. Madagascar settlement patterns show technology determinism: Austronesian maritime technology enabled circumcontinental migration; African land-based technology confined to mainland diffusion. Same island received different populations at different times based on technology access determining possible routes.
