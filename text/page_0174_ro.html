Rezumat

African antelope appear domesticable (vast herds) but cannot be domesticated. Males space into territories and fight fiercely during breeding. Cannot be maintained in crowded captive enclosures like sheep/goats/cattle. Territorial aggression prevents herd domestication. Physical abundance (vast herds) irrelevant without behavioral suitability for captivity.

Antelope genetics: territorial spacing incompatible with crowded enclosures. Contrast: sheep/goats/cattle tolerate crowding through non-territorial genetics.
