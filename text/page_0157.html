Summary

Chapter 9 frames animal domestication question using Anna Karenina analogy: "Happy families all alike; every unhappy family unhappy in own way" becomes "Domesticable animals all alike; every undomesticable animal undomesticable in own way." Framework: domestication requires multiple traits; single missing trait prevents domestication entirely.

Key Concepts

Domestication trait requirements. Successful domestication requires: manageable size, acceptable temperament, suitable diet, social behavior compatible with human management, reproducibility in captivity, disposition to not panic. Missing any single trait prevents domestication entirely despite other advantages.

Historical & Geographic Context

Anna Karenina Principle applies across human history. Thousands of large mammal species examined. Only tiny fraction possess all domestication requirements. Zebras (mentioned in title): untamable temperament despite otherwise suitable. Horses: domesticated. Geographic fauna distribution determines which species possess domestication trait combinations.

Connection to Main Thesis

Domestication genetics geography-distributed. Geographic species pools determine which animals possess domestication trait combinations. Eurasian fauna possessed horses (all traits). African fauna possessed zebras (missing temperament). Americas fauna possessed llamas/alpacas (size/temperament limitations). Geographic animal genetics determine domesticable inventory independent of human effort.
