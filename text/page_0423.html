Summary

Complex emergent properties: one truck driver braking 1 second determines WWII outcome affecting 100 million. Small lower-level changes generate massive higher-level changes. Biological systems ultimately physical obeying quantum mechanics but complexity makes systems effectively unpredictable. Contingency: small events cascade to major effects. History contains irreducible unpredictability despite ultimate determinism.

Key Concepts

Emergence: higher-level properties not predictable from lower-level data. Deterministic systems can be unpredictable practically. Complexity generating unpredictability despite underlying determinism. Small variations cascade large effects. Path dependence: early contingencies constrain later possibilities. History deterministic (follows laws) but practically unpredictable (too many variables).

Historical & Geographic Context

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic determinism compatible with practical unpredictability. Large patterns (geography determines civilization type) predictable.
