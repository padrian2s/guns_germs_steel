Rezumat

New Guinea agricultural limitation: protein deficiency in domesticated staples. Taro (primary staple) contains only 1% protein versus Fertile Crescent wheat (8-14%) and pulses (20-25%). Highland populations suffer protein limitation despite adequate calorie production. Geographic species availability determines crop nutritional content constraints.

Concepte Cheie

Crop nutritional content geography-determined. Wild species genetics determine domesticated cultivar traits. Taro evolution as root crop resulted in low-protein staple. Fertile Crescent grain evolution resulted in high-protein staples. Geographic wild plant species genetics determine agricultural nutrition capacity independent of human technology or effort.
