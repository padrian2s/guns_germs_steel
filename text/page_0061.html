Summary

Islands without major streams developed intensive dryland agriculture using terraces, mulching, crop rotation. This labor-intensive approach became highly productive on Easter, Anuta, Tonga where Polynesians devoted most land to food production. Geographic constraint (no rivers) drove technological adaptation, enabling high population density on small islands.
