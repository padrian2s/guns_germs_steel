Rezumat

New Guinea population fragmented into hundreds/thousands of villages. Each village/microsociety fought fiercely with each other and other language groups. Each microsociety too small to support chiefs, craft specialists, or technologies like metallurgy, writing. Geographic isolation restricted technology/idea inflow. New Guinea's three neighbors (Indonesia, Philippines, Australia) separated by water barriers preventing technology diffusion.

Concepte Cheie

Population fragmentation prevents state-scale organization. Small village scale insufficient for specialization. Geographic isolation blocks technology diffusion. Water barriers prevent contact with neighbors. Two limitations on development: population fragmentation internally and geographic isolation externally.
