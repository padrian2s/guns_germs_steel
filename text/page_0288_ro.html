Rezumat

Spatial realities of band societies: bands meet others at territorial borders periodically exchanging trade items and brides. Territory coordination requires periodic meetings. Densely populated regions make this model impossible: too many populations to maintain periodic border meetings, too much territorial overlap. Conflict resolution, decision-making, economics, and space converge requiring large societies be centralized. Centralization inevitably opens power-holding opportunities for kleptocracy. Spatial pressure necessitates organizational change.

Concepte Cheie

Population density determines governance structure necessity. Sparse populations: bands meet periodically at borders. Dense populations: continuous interaction requires permanent authority. Spatial logistics: high-density requires centralized coordination impossible in egalitarian systems. Conflict resolution: frequent territorial disputes demand authority mediating. Trade coordination: dense populations require efficient administration. Each factor geometry-determined: density level determines feasible governance model.
