Rezumat

Technology adoption barriers: RCA vacuum tube example (reluctance to compete with own products). British gas lighting into 1920s despite US/German electric adoption. Why? British municipal governments heavily invested in gas infrastructure; regulatory obstacles against competing electric companies. Institutional investment in old technology blocks new technology despite technical superiority.

Concepte Cheie

Technology adoption depends on infrastructure investment, not purely on technology merit. Institutions investing in existing systems resist new competitors. Regulatory power determines technology adoption rates. Geographic administrative structures (municipal control, regulatory authority) determine which societies can rapidly adopt superior technologies. Entrenched infrastructure creates technology lock-in despite better alternatives available.
