Rezumat

Late or absent agriculture in Australia and Americas resulted from geometric constraints, not cultural factors. Megafauna extinctions eliminated large game animals from Americas/Australia (but not Eurasia/Africa). Poor hunting resources paradoxically would reduce competition for agriculture, yet agriculture delayed/absent. Geographic fauna loss determines region's domesticable animal availability.

Concepte Cheie

Megafauna extinction geography-determined. Americas and Australia: ice-age megafauna extinct. Eurasia and Africa: megafauna survived. Agriculture lacks domesticable large animals in species-poor regions. Geographic timing of megafauna extinction determines present-day animal availability for domestication.
