Summary

Archaeological dating challenges: materials from different time periods get mixed through soil disturbance. Accelerator mass spectrometry allows precise radiocarbon dating of individual plant/animal remains, solving chronological ambiguities in establishing domestication timing.

Key Concepts

Dating methodology. Radiocarbon dating calibration necessary for accuracy. Precise dating of domesticated remains establishes geographic variation in agricultural adoption timing. Chronological precision reveals independent invention versus diffusion patterns.

Historical & Geographic Context

Archaeological methodology improved dating precision over recent decades. Early-mid 20th century: crude chronologies. Late 20th century: accelerator mass spectrometry enables precise individual-specimen dating. Better dating confirms multiple independent agricultural centers.

Connection to Main Thesis

Precise archaeological dating reveals geographic variation in agriculture adoption timing. Different regions developed agriculture independently at different times. Geographic conditions determine agricultural emergence timing. Dating methodology proves geographic determinism central to agricultural history.
