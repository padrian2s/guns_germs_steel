Summary

Why did farmers defeat hunters? The answer lies in abundance: agriculture produces more calories per hectare than hunting-gathering, enabling larger populations. For 7 million years post-divergence, all humans foraged. But agriculture's caloric advantage altered the power balance irreversibly.

Key Concepts

Caloric productivity. Agricultural societies support 10-100x higher population density than hunter-gatherers. More people = more soldiers, craftspeople, administrators. Numerical advantage compounds technological advantage.

Historical & Geographic Context

Seven million years of human evolution: all humans hunted and gathered. Agricultural transition occurred recently (last 13,000 years) and unevenly. Early adoption in Fertile Crescent ~10,000 BCE. Later or never in many Americas regions.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic distribution of domesticable species determines agricultural adoption timing. Regions with suitable wild plants/animals transitioned to farming earlier.
