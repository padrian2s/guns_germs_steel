Summary

Australia/New Guinea represented apparent disadvantage: smallest continent, mostly desert, later occupied than Africa/Eurasia, isolated. Key Concepts

Island colonization: hitherto unprecedented golden age of population explosions. Repeated cycles: colonize island, adapt, expand to next island. Selective pressure hypothesis: Great Leap Forward emerged here, spread back to Old World. Alternative geographic advantage mechanism.

Historical & Geographic Context

Australia/New Guinea colonization 40,000-30,000 years ago. Island maze of Indonesia offered unique colonization pattern. Watercraft technology developed early. Isolation afterward prevented advantages from transferring elsewhere.
