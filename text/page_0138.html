Summary

Self-compatible hermaphroditic plants provide reproductive convenience for farmers. High percentage of self-compatible plants in Fertile Crescent flora aided early farmers. Self-compatible plants occasionally cross-pollinate creating new varieties for selection. Both asexual reproduction (roots duplicating parents) and occasional sexual crossing enabled trait diversity.

Key Concepts

Reproductive biology advantages. Self-compatibility enabling propagation without genetic engineering. Cross-pollination providing genetic diversity for selection. Asexual reproduction enabling cloning of superior plants. Geographic flora reproductive characteristics determining domestication ease. High self-compatible species concentration advantageous.

Historical & Geographic Context

Fertile Crescent: high self-compatible species percentage facilitated domestication. Wheat, barley, peas self-compatible. Farmers easily saved seeds from superior plants. Occasional cross-pollination created variation. Mediterranean region: many self-incompatible species (apples, pears, cherries) requiring complex reproductive management.
