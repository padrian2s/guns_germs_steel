Summary

This page presents the table of contents, outlining Diamond's four-part structure: the question (Prologue), evidence from history before 1500 CE (Part One), the origins of agriculture and technology (Parts Two-Three), and regional case studies (Part Four).

Key Concepts

The structure reveals Diamond's methodology: establish the question (why inequality?), prove that technological differences existed by 1500 CE, trace these differences to food production advantages, then demonstrate the pattern across different continents. Chapter titles emphasize geographical determinism: "Collision at Cajamarca," "Farmer Power," "Spacious Skies and Tilted Axes."

Historical & Geographic Context

The 1500 CE date marks Europe's expansion, making it the pivot point for examining pre-existing technological gaps. Diamond organizes content around three mechanisms: food production's role, domestication of plants and animals, and geographic axes affecting technology transfer.
