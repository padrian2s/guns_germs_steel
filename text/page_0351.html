Summary

New Guineans expanded westward against Austronesian tide into eastern Indonesia. North Halmahera/Timor islands contain Papuan languages (related to western New Guinea languages, not Austronesian). Key Concepts

Food production technology determines expansion outcomes. Superior technology enables replacement; comparable technology enables coexistence/competition. Language distribution documents competitive history. Population boundaries reflect technology balance. Geographic regions with indigenous advantages show indigenous expansion/persistence.

Historical & Geographic Context

Indonesia: mixture of Austronesian and Papuan language regions reflecting differential expansion outcomes. Austronesian successful west/Philippines. Papuan languages surviving/expanding east (Halmahera, Timor) suggests more challenging terrain for Austronesian expansion. Geographic variation in terrain/indigenous capacity created variable expansion outcomes. Papuan expansion westward indicates local advantage in island geography despite Austronesian maritime advantage.
