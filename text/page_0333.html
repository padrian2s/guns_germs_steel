Key Concepts

Hunter-gatherer survival in agricultural dominated regions through trade/symbiosis. Language adoption follows close contact/trade without full agricultural adoption. Minority populations adopt majority language as lingua franca. Trade creates cultural diffusion (language) without requiring economic conversion. Indigenous populations persist through economic specialization (hunting providing goods agricultural populations want). Language prestige causes adoption despite subsistence difference.
