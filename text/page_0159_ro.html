Rezumat

Only 14 large herbivorous mammal species (>100 lbs) domesticated before 20th century. Five major: sheep, goat, cow, pig, horse. Nine minor: Arabian camel, Bactrian camel, llama/alpaca, donkey, reindeer, water buffalo, yak, banteng, gaur. Extreme rarity: thousands of large mammals, only 14 domesticated. Geographic distribution of these 14 highly uneven.

Concepte Cheie

Domestication extreme rarity. Most large mammals refuse domestication despite thousands of years effort. Genetic domestication traits: favorable temperament, acceptable size, appropriate diet, social hierarchy, rapid maturation, willingness to breed in captivity. Most species fail on one or more criteria. Only 14 succeed globally.

Context Istoric È™i Geografic

Ancient Fourteen: mostly Eurasian/North African origin. Sheep, goat, cow, pig: Fertile Crescent. Horse: Eurasian steppes. Camels: Middle East/North Africa. Americas: only llama/alpaca (Andes mountains). Africa: only guinea fowl beyond Eurasian species introduction. Australia: zero large domesticable mammals. Uneven geographic distribution.

Americas: zero major, only two minor and geographically constrained. Australia: zero. Geographic fauna distribution determines animal domestication inventory. Uneven distribution explains Eurasian technology advantage through available large mammal domestication.
