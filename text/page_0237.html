Summary

Writing adoption conditions: food production necessary but not sufficient condition. Food production enables complex political organization required for writing but doesn't guarantee writing adoption. Some food-producing societies with complex political organizations failed to develop or adopt writing before modern times. Geographic diffusion of writing: spread through trade, conquest, religion to societies with similar economic/political organization. Writing spread to compatible societies; societies lacking compatible organization resist writing adoption.

Connection to Main Thesis

Writing adoption geography-determined: food production necessary, but geographic proximity to existing writing systems determines adoption. Writing diffusion geography-determined: trade networks, geographic proximity, political alliance determine spread. Societies receiving writing via geographic diffusion gain administrative advantages.
