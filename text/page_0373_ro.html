Rezumat

Spanish colonization succeeded where Norse failed due to source, target, latitude, and time. Spain (unlike Norway) wealthy and populous supporting exploration and colonial subsidies. Spanish landfalls subtropical latitudes highly suitable food production. Geographic alignment enabled European advantages effective application. Colonization success requires matching source population capability with target geography suitable for European agriculture.

Concepte Cheie

Colonization success multi-factor geographic: source population wealth (exploration funding), source population size (settler numbers), target latitude (agriculture suitability), target climate (European crop compatibility). Spain wealthy/populous enabling exploration investment. Spain temperate climate origin enables temperate agriculture exporting to similar climates. Subtropical Americas suitable European crops (wheat, barley, sugar) enabling profitable agriculture. Wealth generation supports colonial control establishment supports settlement expansion.

Spain 1492: wealthy from 700 years Mediterranean trade, powerful centralizing state, population >8 million. Spanish economy enabled exploration funding, colonial military subsidies, agricultural investment. Caribbean/Mesoamerica: subtropical climate suitable European crops, disease environment already endemic (malaria/yellow fever history), native populations densely settled (conquest opportunities). Norse 1000 AD: Norway population ~200,000, poor subsistence economy, Arctic targets, limited exploration resources. Spanish geographic origin (Mediterranean wealth economy) enabled wealth accumulation enabling colonization. Spanish target geography (subtropical agriculture suitability) enabled profitable settlement. Norse origin poverty and Arctic target unsuitability caused failure.

Source homeland climate determines agricultural compatibility with target. Target latitude determines agriculture suitability for source population. Target disease ecology determines settlement ease. Spain gained geographic advantages: Mediterranean trade origin wealth enabled exploration; temperate agriculture suitability in subtropics enabled profitable settlement; target disease already endemic limited new epidemics. Norse lacked advantages: northern poverty limited funding; Arctic unsuitability prevented agriculture prevented profitable settlement. Colonial outcome geography-determined: favorable geographic alignment (Spain+subtropics) succeeded; unfavorable alignment (Norse+Arctic) failed.
