Summary

Marine resources vary: most islands surrounded by shallow reefs/lagoons rich in fish/shellfish. Easter, Pitcairn, Marquesas have rocky coasts without reefsâ€”marine-poor. Mountain-rich islands (Hawaii) have rain-generated streams enabling irrigation agriculture. Lower islands (Tonga, Easter) have rich volcanic soils. Geological variation determines available resources for subsistence and tools.

Key Concepts

Geology determines resource availability. Coral atolls: no freshwater, thin soil. Volcanic islands: diverse stone tools available. Continental fragments: mineral-rich resources. Marine productivity varies by reef presence. Mountains generate streams for irrigation. All determine food production capacity and craft specialization.

Historical & Geographic Context

Polynesian islands: geologic diversity from bare atolls to continental New Zealand. Marine environments: reef-rich to rocky coasts. Rainfall/streams: abundant to scarce. All factors vary independently, creating unique combinations on each island.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geologic factors determine: available stone tools, subsistence productivity, freshwater availability, agriculture suitability. These determine population size, specialization, organizational complexity. Geographic substrate directly produces societal variation observed across Polynesia.
