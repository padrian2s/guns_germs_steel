Summary

Agricultural diffusion wave mapped from Fertile Crescent chronologically. Greece/Cyprus: 6500 BCE. Egypt: 6000 BCE. Central Europe: 5400 BCE. Southern Spain: 5200 BCE. Britain: 3500 BCE. Diffusion radiated east-west following similar-latitude zones. Regional food production adoption timing reflects geographic distance/latitude compatibility from origin. Nearer regions adopted earlier; farther regions adopted later.

Key Concepts

Diffusion wave geographic patterning. East-west diffusion enables rapid spread (similar climate/season). Timing follows geographic distance/compatibility. Earlier regions: nearby latitudes, climate-compatible (Egypt, Greece). Later regions: distant or different latitude (Britain, Spain). Diffusion speed geography-determined through latitude/climate factors. Central Europe middle position reflects intermediate distance.

Historical & Geographic Context

Fertile Crescent origin ~10000 BCE. Neighboring regions adopted quickly: Egypt, Mediterranean, Iraq ~6000 BCE. European diffusion slower northward: Greece ~6500 BCE, Spain ~5200 BCE, Britain ~3500 BCE. 3000-year lag Britain adoption reflects geographic distance and climate gradient northward. Diffusion speed ~1km/year from origin. East-west diffusion enabled rapid Mediterranean spread.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic diffusion patterns prove geographic determinism. Adoption timing geography-determined through distance/latitude. Fertile Crescent advantage cascaded through diffusion wave. Early-adopting regions (Egypt, Greece, Mesopotamia) achieved civilizational advantages before later-adopting regions (Britain, Spain). Geographic distance determines developmental timing. Contiguous east-west regions developed together; distant/north-south regions lagged. Diffusion patterns geography-determined.
