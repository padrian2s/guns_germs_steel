Rezumat

Artificial selection through harvesting dramatically alters crop morphology. Supermarket apples: 3 inches diameter vs. 1 inch wild. Corn cobs: 0.5 inches original vs. 6 inches by 1500 AD vs. 18 inches modern. Bitterness reduction through selection. Fleshiness increase (squash, pumpkins) from seed-heavy wild ancestors. Size increases arise from genetic variation in wild populations.

Concepte Cheie

Genetic variation in wild crops enables artificial selection. Humans unconsciously selecting larger seeds/fruits through harvesting. Bitterness reduction through repeated harvesting. Size accumulation over generations from available genetic variation. Different regions had different wild crop genetic diversity determining achievable improvements.
