Summary

Agricultural diffusion: Southwest Asian founder crops/animals spread to Europe (~6000-3500 BCE). Local wild plants subsequently domesticated. Europe's poppy, oats domesticated locally after founder package established agriculture foundation. Geographic proximity to Fertile Crescent enabled agricultural adoption through diffusion + local innovation.

Key Concepts

Agricultural diffusion patterns. Founder crops establish agricultural system, enable local domestication of previously unsuitable wild species. Geographic barriers determine diffusion speed. Climate similarity enables successful crop adoption. Transportation networks enable crop/animal spread across continents.

Historical & Geographic Context

Southwest Asia founded 10000 BCE. Europe received agriculture 4000-5500 years later. Mediterranean climates favorable to crop diffusion. Poppy confined to coastal western Mediterranean - local domestication after agriculture arrived. Geographic proximity to innovation source crucial.
