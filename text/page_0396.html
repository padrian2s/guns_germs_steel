Key Concepts

Historical & Geographic Context

Bantu northern competition: faced established farming populations with iron metallurgy, dense settlements, political organization. Slow northward expansion encountering resistance. Southern opportunity: Khoisan hunter-gatherers sparse population, no iron, no crops, no organized states. Rapid southward expansion 2000 miles to Natal achieving conquest within centuries. Expansion route selection geography-determined: easier southern route chosen over harder northern competition.
