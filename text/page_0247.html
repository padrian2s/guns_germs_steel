Summary

Technology trial/error illustrated by gunpowder/gasoline development. Combustible natural products inevitably noticed (resinous logs in fires). Mesopotamians 2000 BCE extracting petroleum heating rock asphalt (tons annually). Ancient Greeks discovered petroleum/pitch/resin/sulfur/quicklime mixtures as incendiary weapons. Geographic fuel availability determined who could develop which technologies.

Key Concepts

Natural material availability determines technology experimentation possibility. Geographic resource access enables/limits technology development. Combustible materials discovery serendipitous but geographic distribution non-random. Regions with natural fuel resources more likely developing fuel-based technologies.
