Rezumat

Communal decision-making viable only with certain population sizes. Small New Guinea villages enable full-community meetings: information spreads quickly to all, all hear each other, all who want speak have opportunity. As populations grow, these prerequisites become impossible: information can't reach everyone quickly, meeting forums too large for mutual hearing, speaking opportunities limited. Larger populations require representative systems or hierarchical decision-making. Population scale determines whether egalitarian governance mechanically feasible.

Concepte Cheie

Egalitarian governance population-limited by communication constraints. Villages ~100-500: all-adult meetings still feasible. Villages 1000+: information dispersal prevents universal knowledge. Large cities millions: direct democracy impossible, representation/hierarchy necessary. Population scale directly determines governance mechanism feasibility. Hierarchy emerges not from cultural choice but communication necessity: large populations require delegated authority. Geographic carrying capacity determines population scale determining governance feasibility.
