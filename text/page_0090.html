Summary

Hunter-gatherer societies: necessarily egalitarian, lack full-time bureaucrats, small-scale tribal organization because all able-bodied members must spend most time acquiring food. Agricultural societies: food can be stored/stockpiled, allowing elite to control surplus, implement taxation, escape food production, pursue specialization.

Food storage â†’ hierarchy. Agricultural surplus enables: food taxation, elite emergence, craft specialization, military formation, bureaucratic administration. Hunter-gatherers cannot accumulate enough surplus to support non-producing elites or armies. Fundamental structural difference.

Historical & Geographic Context

Fertile Crescent societies (3000 BCE onward) developed hierarchical kingdoms based on grain storage. Egypt, Mesopotamia, China: organized around agricultural surplus taxation. Hunter-gatherer societies worldwide: egalitarian band/tribe organization persisted because surplus impossible.
