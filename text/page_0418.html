Summary

Alternative explanation: local cultural factors unrelated environment. Minor cultural feature arising trivial temporary local reasons becomes fixed. Fixed features predispose society toward important cultural choices later. Chaos theory application: small contingencies amplify major outcomes. Such cultural processes history's "wild cards" making history unpredictable. Culture contingency potentially undermines geographic determinism.

Key Concepts

Cultural contingency: minor decisions fixed become major constraints. Path dependence: early arbitrary choices determine later possibilities. Chaos theory: small contingencies cascade to large effects. Cultural factors possibly independent geography creating historical unpredictability. Non-deterministic mechanisms possibly operating alongside geographic determinism.

Historical & Geographic Context

QWERTY keyboard: arbitrary design but fixation created persistent suboptimal technology. Cultural lock-in: technology standards adopted for arbitrary reasons persist. Language quirks: Chinese homophones maybe affect literacy development but geography didn't select homophones. Culture possibly operates independently geography.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic determinism incomplete: culture contingency adds non-deterministic element. Geography sets constraints but culture within constraints makes unpredictable choices. Geography determines framework; culture determines specific path within framework. Full historical understanding requires both geographic constraints and cultural contingency acknowledging some history irreducibly unpredictable.
