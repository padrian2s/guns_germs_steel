Key Concepts

Observational sciences vs. experimental sciences. Observational constraints require alternative methods (comparison, natural experiments). Proximate causes (immediate mechanism) vs. ultimate causes (why mechanism evolved/operates). Teleological explanation: structure exists for function. Function-based explanation essential biology/history (evolutionary logic).

Historical & Geographic Context

Astronomy: observational, cannot experiment stars. Geology: observational, cannot recreate earth history. Ecology: observational, limited experimentation. History: observational, cannot recreate civilizations. All require comparison and natural experiments (varying conditions in nature). Proximate/ultimate distinction: agriculture technology causes civilization (proximate), but geography causes agriculture (ultimate). Understanding requires both causal levels.

Connection to Main Thesis

Historic science possible through observational methodology. Proximate causes (technology, institutions) traceable to ultimate causes (geography). Comparing societies with differing geography enables pattern observation. If geographic patterns consistent, history becomes testable science. Proximate-ultimate analysis enables mechanism explanation enables science without experimentation.
