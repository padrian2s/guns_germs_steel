Summary

Crop diffusion geography-limited by climate barriers. Similar climates enable rapid diffusion: Fertile Crescent crops south to Ethiopia (similar rainfall), Bantu crops Great Lakes to Natal (comparable climate). Different climate zones prevent diffusion: Indonesia tropical crops impossible reach Australia. Shorter distance diffusion also fails across climate boundaries: Mexico crops slow adoption US Southwest/Southeast (arid climate).

Key Concepts

Climate-limited diffusion. Crops adapted to source climate fail in incompatible climates despite distance shortness. Geography determines crop compatibility. Latitude barriers more significant than longitude distance. Similar latitude = successful diffusion. Different latitude = diffusion failure. Continental shape affects diffusion patterns.

Historical & Geographic Context

Mediterranean crops spread north-south effectively within Mediterranean/temperate zones. Tropical crops cannot spread to temperate zones (climate incompatibility). Mexico crops required adaptation for US climates (thousand-year lag). New World vs Old World crop patterns geography-determined by latitude distribution.

Connection to Main Thesis

Crop diffusion geography-constrained. Climate similarity enables diffusion; incompatibility prevents it. Distance secondary to climate compatibility. Continental climate diversity enables independent domestication. Geographic latitude distribution determines diffusion feasibility. Climate geography ultimately controls which crops adopted where despite technology/knowledge spread.
