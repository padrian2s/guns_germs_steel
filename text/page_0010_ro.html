Rezumat

Diamond argues that conventional histories omit non-Western societies and neglect pre-literate history (99.9% of human existence). He contends that by 3,000 BCE, Eurasian societies possessed cities, governments, metal tools, domesticated animals, and agriculture while other continents lacked these technologies.

Concepte Cheie

Diamond identifies three flaws in conventional world history: (1) ignoring the majority world population; (2) focusing only on post-3000 BCE "literate" history; (3) missing the fundamental question of why these disparities originated. He argues technological inequality by 1500 CE had deeper roots extending into prehistory.
