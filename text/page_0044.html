Key Concepts

Uniform extinction across diverse habitats indicates human cause, not climate. Climate hypothesis predicts habitat-dependent extinction. Overkill hypothesis: humans directly responsible through hunting and environmental modification. Geographic uniformity proves human agency.

Historical & Geographic Context

Australian megafauna extinction ~40,000 years ago correlated with human colonization. All habitat zones affected equally despite diverse climates. Pattern repeated across newly colonized islands and continents.
