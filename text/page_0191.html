Summary

Eurasian agriculture spread faster than Native American or sub-Saharan African agriculture. Wheat/barley spread Atlantic to Pacific across Eurasia's east-west axis. Sub-Saharan agriculture north-south spread slower (Egypt to South Africa never achieved). Americas north-south agricultural diffusion slow (Mexico to US Southwest, thousands of years). Faster Eurasian agricultural diffusion cascaded into faster technology diffusion: writing, metallurgy, technology, empires.

Key Concepts

East-west axis geography enables rapid agricultural diffusion. Similar climates across continent allow crop/animal spread. North-south diffusion blocked by climate gradients. Eurasian east-west span enables rapid technology cascade. Technology diffusion rate determined by geographic axis orientation. Agricultural spread speed determines writing/metallurgy/empire spread speed.

Historical & Geographic Context

Eurasian wheat/barley spread: Atlantic to Pacific across temperate zone rapidly. Sub-Saharan agriculture: Egypt to South Africa impossible (tropical interruption). Americas maize: Mexico to US Southwest took thousands of years (north-south climate gradient). Americas geography blocked domesticate diffusion speed. Eurasian geography enabled domesticate diffusion speed. Geographic axis determines diffusion rate independent of distance.

Connection to Main Thesis

Eurasian east-west axis geography cascades into rapid agricultural diffusion. Rapid agricultural diffusion cascades into rapid technology diffusion (writing, metallurgy, empires). Americas north-south axis geography cascades into slow agricultural diffusion. Slow agricultural diffusion cascades into slow technology diffusion. Geographic axis orientation determines civilizational technology levels by 1500 CE. Geography, not human biology, determines technology disparities.
