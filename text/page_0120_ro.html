Rezumat

Plant domestication involved multiple morphological changes. First: seed dispersal mechanisms. Wild plants have specialized dispersal (seeds scatter, preventing human harvest). Only mutant seeds lacking dispersal could be harvested efficiently. Humans inadvertently selected for non-dispersing seeds through harvesting practices.

Concepte Cheie

Artificial selection through harvesting. Humans selecting mutant plants with favorable traits (non-dispersing seeds, larger seeds, reduced bitterness). Selection pressures different between wild and cultivated conditions. Unconscious artificial selection created crop differences gradually.
