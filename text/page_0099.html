Summary

Multiple independent agricultural centers identified globally (Figure 5.1). Fertile Crescent, China, Mesoamerica, Ethiopia, New Guinea, Americas regions show early food production. Question marks indicate uncertainty whether development independent or influenced by diffusion from other centers. Archaeological evidence maps agricultural emergence geography.

Key Concepts

Multiple independent agricultural origins. Geographic variation in domestication emergence. Independent invention versus diffusion patterns. Agricultural development appears independent in isolated geographic regions. Geographic separation enabled parallel agricultural discovery in multiple centers.

Historical & Geographic Context

Agricultural origins: Fertile Crescent 10000 BCE, China 9000 BCE, Mesoamerica 5000 BCE, Ethiopia, New Guinea, sub-Saharan Africa, Americas independently. Each region's agriculture based on locally available domesticable species. Geographic isolation enabled multiple independent discoveries.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic isolation determines independent agricultural origin versus diffusion pattern. Multiple centers confirm agriculture invention wasn't unique discovery but inevitable result of having domesticable species available.
