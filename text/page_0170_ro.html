Rezumat

Cheetah domestication failure: example of genetic impossibility. Ancient Egypt/Assyria/modern India: cheetahs desired for hunting (superior to dogs). Mogul emperor: kept 1000 cheetahs stable. Wealthy prince investments: thousands of years, enormous resources. Result: all cheetahs remained wild, never domesticated. Genetic traits preventing domestication despite intense human motivation.

Concepte Cheie

Economic motivation insufficient for domestication. Intense desire (hunting advantage) can't overcome genetic impossibility. Taming achievable; domestication impossible. Cheetahs lack genetic docility, breeding compatibility, fertility traits. No selection possible (cheetahs in captivity won't breed). Genetic constraints trump human effort.
