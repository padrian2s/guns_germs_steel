Rezumat

Diseases rapidly evolve antigen variants. Influenza changes annually: immune system ineffective against new strains. Malaria/sleeping sickness antigenically slippery. AIDS most slippery: evolves new antigens within infected host. Natural selection favors antigenic variation in pathogens. Host immune system continuously defeated by microbial adaptation. Natural selection defense: human genetic change across generations.

Concepte Cheie

Microbial natural selection faster than human genetic selection. Antigenic evolution exceeds immune adaptation rate. Arms race: microbes evolve variants faster than immunity evolves. Population immunity lag enables reinfection. Dense populations accelerate antigenic selection pressure. Geographic population density determines microbial evolution rate.
