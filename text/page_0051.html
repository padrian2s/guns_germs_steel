Summary

Maori in New Zealand adapted to local conditions fast: discovered stone sources within century, killed all moas within centuries, created diverse societies from coastal hunter-gatherers to farmers in few centuries. This rapid adaptation means Africa's head start wouldn't protect against American advantage. Americas' larger area (50% greater than Africa's) and greater environmental diversity would favor Native Americans over Africans within a millennium.

Key Concepts

Human adaptability rapid once modern behavioral capacity present. Geographic factors matter: area size, environmental diversity favor development. Early settlement doesn't guarantee lasting advantage if geographic factors unfavorable.

Historical & Geographic Context

Maori colonization New Zealand ~1000 CE; rapid diversification. New Zealand smaller, less diverse than Americas. New Zealand development trajectory less expansive than Americas offered potential.

Connection to Main Thesis

Continental size and diversity matter more than time occupied. Americas' geographic advantages could outweigh late settlement. This foreshadows later revelation: geographic factors beyond timing (domesticable species availability, continental axis, climate zones) determine civilization development pace.
