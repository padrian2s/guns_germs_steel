Summary

Bantu farmers from West African savanna expanded into wetter coastal forests by 3000 BCE. Archaeological/linguistic evidence shows: cattle, yams (wet-climate crops), no metals, fishing-hunting-gathering coexistence. Tsetse fly disease eliminated cattle in rainforests. Bantu technology adapted wet-forest zones, continuing expansion into Congo Basin. Environment shaped expansion technology adaptation.

Historical & Geographic Context

Bantu homeland savanna: cattle herding economy supplemented crops. Forest expansion (3000 BCE): rainforest environment unsuitable cattle (tsetse flies). Bantu response: loss of cattle maintaining crops (yams, manioc, plantains). Fishing/hunting/gathering supplemented agriculture. Continued expansion into Congo Basin maintained agriculture through wet-climate crops. Resource availability determined Bantu diet composition and economy structure.
