Rezumat

Chapter 6 question: Why did agriculture adoption vary by region and timing? Mediterranean Fertile Crescent: ~8500 BCE. Mediterranean southwestern Europe: ~5500 BCE (3000 years later). Mediterranean California, Australia, southern Africa: never adopted agriculture indigenously. Same climate, different adoption timing/outcome. What geographic factors explain variation?

Concepte Cheie

Agricultural adoption variation. Geographic similarity (Mediterranean climates) yet dramatically different outcomes. Why did California never develop agriculture despite Mediterranean climate supporting wild plant abundance? Answer: domesticable wild species distribution. Lack of suitable domesticable plants/animals = no independent agriculture development possible.

Context Istoric È™i Geografic

Fertile Crescent ~8500 BCE: wild wheat, barley, lentils, animals available. Mediterranean Europe: received crops via diffusion ~5500 BCE. California: wild acorns abundant, yet no large-seeded grasses (wild grass seeds tiny). Australia, southern Africa: similar - no domesticable crops available. Climate irrelevant without domesticable species.

Species availability, not climate, determines agriculture feasibility.
