Bibliography continued: major technology histories. Joseph Needham "Science and Civilisation in China" - 5 volumes in 16 parts (1954+) with dozen more planned. Comprehensive Chinese technology history. Ahmad al-Hassan/Donald Hill "Islamic Technology" (1992), K. D. White "Greek and Roman Technology" (1984). Scholarly works documenting technology development across major civilizations. Support for geographic determinism: technology development traced through available resources and geographic constraints across regions.

Key Concepts

Comprehensive technology histories document regional development patterns. Chinese technology: silk production, printing, gunpowder, porcelain - enabled by geographic silk worm habitat, available materials. Islamic technology: mathematics, astronomy, engineering - enabled by trade networks geographic position. Greek/Roman technology: metallurgy, engineering, military - enabled by Mediterranean geography and resources. Technology regional variation reflects geographic resource availability.

Historical & Geographic Context

China technology history: silk production geographic-unique (silkworm native East Asia). Gunpowder from sulfur/saltpeter availability. Printing from paper technology development. Islamic technology: position at crossroads enabled accumulation of multiple traditions. Greek/Roman technology: Mediterranean bronze/iron resources, Mediterranean connectivity enabling diffusion. Connection to Main Thesis

Technology histories prove geographic determinism across civilizations. Needham's Chinese works document silk (geographic advantage), gunpowder (geographic mineral availability), printing (paper technology). Al-Hassan/Hill document Islamic technology geographic position advantage. White documents Greco-Roman technology from Mediterranean resource availability. Technology development geography-determined through available resources, geographic trade connectivity, and geographic raw materials. Comprehensive technology histories provide evidence base: each civilization's technological achievements reflect geographic advantages available to that region.
