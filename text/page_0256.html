Historical & Geographic Context

Transistor diffusion: post-WWII US-Japan trade routes enabled rapid peaceful technology transfer. Silkworms: Byzantine Empire espionage from China through Persia, required covert geographic smuggling routes. Huguenots: geography of religious persecution (France) forced emigration creating technology transfer vectors to receiving societies (Protestant Northern Europe, colonies). Medieval Islamic world: geographic position at intersection of Asian-European-African trade enabled technology aggregation from all continents.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic connectivity determines technology diffusion pathway available. Continental position along major trade routes (Islamic Middle East, Medieval Europe) enables rapid peaceful diffusion. Geographic isolation (Australia, New Guinea) blocks peaceful diffusion unless espionage succeeds. Refugee emigration primarily follows geographic safety corridors. Military conquest follows geographic feasibility. Geography determines not just whether technology transfers but which transfer mechanism becomes viable, affecting adoption speed and completeness.
