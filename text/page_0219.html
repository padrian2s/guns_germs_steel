Summary

Figure 12.1: Writing origins timeline with question marks. Egypt/China writing independence uncertain: possibly independent or stimulated by earlier systems elsewhere. Evidence ambiguity: determining whether invention independent vs diffusion-prompted. Writing diffusion across civilizations more likely than parallel invention. Technology transfer: more probable than simultaneous re-invention.

Key Concepts

Writing diffusion vs independent invention. Geographic contact determines diffusion likelihood. Writing system complex enough that parallel invention unlikely. Trade contact geography determines diffusion pathways. Civilizations in geographic proximity: likely writing diffusion. Geographically isolated regions: genuine independent invention more likely.

Historical & Geographic Context

Mesopotamia/Egypt proximity: writing possibly diffused Egypt from Sumeria. Egypt geography: Mediterranean trade contact enabled diffusion. China geographic isolation: independent writing invention plausible. Americas geographic isolation: writing development (Mayan, Aztec) independent from Old World. Geographic trade patterns determine diffusion likelihood for complex technologies.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geography determines trade contact. Trade contact determines technology diffusion. Isolated geography: enables genuine independent invention. Connected geography: encourages technology diffusion. Writing emergence: geography-determined (through contact patterns determining diffusion vs invention). Tech diffusion benefits: geographic contact determines who benefits from others' inventions. Geographic position determines innovation copying capability.
