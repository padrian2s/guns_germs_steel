Summary

This page concludes the table of contents, listing final chapters on regional histories (China, Polynesia, Americas/Eurasia comparison, Africa), an epilogue on history as science, and supporting materials (acknowledgments, readings, index).

Key Concepts

Titles like "How China Became Chinese" and "How Africa Became Black" personify continents as agents shaped by history. "Hemispheres Colliding" emphasizes geography's collision point. The epilogue's focus on "History as a Science" indicates Diamond's methodological ambition.

Historical & Geographic Context

These regional chapters span thousands of years across all inhabited continents. Each chapter tests whether the geographical determinism thesis explains different regions' trajectories across Africa, Asia, Polynesia, and the Americas.

Connection to Main Thesis

By ending with an epilogue on history as science, Diamond elevates his geographical explanation beyond narrative to scientific principle. The book's architecture proves that geography acts as a universal causal force across all human societies and time periods.
