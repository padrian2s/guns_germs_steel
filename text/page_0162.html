Summary

Ancient Fourteen concentration in Eurasia explained by: (1) Eurasia has largest number of big terrestrial wild mammal species globally, and (2) Eurasia lost fewest candidate species to extinction in last 40,000 years. Geography determines present-day fauna inventory through both initial species richness and extinction history.

Key Concepts

Geographic fauna abundance. "Candidate for domestication": terrestrial herbivorous/omnivorous mammal species >100 lbs. Eurasia species richness advantage: geography determined larger starting fauna pool. Megafauna survival: Eurasian fauna survived Ice Age, Americas/Australia lost megafauna. Current fauna reflects both geography-provided starting pool and extinction history.

Historical & Geographic Context

Present-day fauna distribution reflects Ice Age extinctions. Americas: mammoths, horses, camels, giant ground sloths extinct ~10,000 BCE. Australia: megafauna extinct ~20,000 BCE. Eurasia/Africa: megafauna survived to present. Result: Eurasia maintained highest candidate species pool through current era. Present-day domestication patterns reflect this historic extinction asymmetry.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic deep-time fauna history determines present-day domestication capacity. Ice Age megafauna extinctions varied by continent (Americas/Australia hit hard, Eurasia/Africa spared).
