Rezumat

Diamond critiques decades of IQ research by white American psychologists attempting to prove African Americans genetically inferior to European Americans. He argues these efforts fail because compared populations differ vastly in social environment and educational opportunities, making it impossible to isolate genetic factors. IQ tests measure cultural learning and childhood opportunities, not "pure innate intelligence."

Concepte Cheie

Diamond distinguishes between genetic intelligence and culturally-shaped abilities. Childhood environment profoundly affects brain development and IQ test performance. The scientific inability to isolate genetic intelligence from environmental factors undermines racist claims about genetic racial differences.

The 1960s-1990s saw contentious debate over IQ differences between racial groups. Psychologist Arthur Jensen and others claimed genetic racial differences in intelligence. Diamond's critique reflects late-20th-century scientific consensus that environment confounds such studies.
