Summary

Geographic variation in large-seeded wild grass species: world's 56 heaviest-seeded wild grasses concentrated in specific regions. Grain weight: 10-40 milligrams (10x global median). Large seeds worth harvesting, supporting cultivation transition. Geographic distribution of large-seeded grasses determines agricultural potential.

Key Concepts

Seed size and domesticability. Large seeds more calorie-valuable, less effort to harvest. World's largest wild grasses geographically clustered. Fertile Crescent: abundance of large-seeded grasses (wheat, barley). Mesoamerica: teosinte smaller-seeded. Sub-Saharan Africa: limited large-seeded grasses. Geographic distribution of optimal species determines agricultural viability.

Historical & Geographic Context

Mediterranean/Near East: 56 heaviest grass species concentrated. Southwest Asia, North Africa dominated world's large-seeded grasses. Americas had no large-seeded native grasses (maize is smaller-seeded). Australia: no large-seeded grasses suitable for domestication.
