Summary

Fertile Crescent wild grasses analyzed: 23 most palatable, largest-seeded candidates identified. Barley and emmer wheat among top candidates. Not all 23 equally useful: barley, emmer wheat superior by multiple criteria. Emmer wheat largest seeds. Domestication selected naturally from wild population: geography determined which plants available to domesticate. Best crops geography-determined through wild species characteristics.

Historical & Geographic Context

Fertile Crescent: 23 wild grass candidates, barley and emmer wheat emerged superior. Large seed size: more nutrition per seed. Reliable growth: predictable yields. Americas: wild grains inferior (maize ancestral teosinte). Tropical Americas: root crops (manioc, potatoes) instead of grains. Africa: sorghum, millet later development. Geography's wild plant resources determined agriculture foundation crops in each region.
