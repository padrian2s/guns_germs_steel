Summary

Polynesian variation ranges from simple personal utensils to monumental stone architecture. Six environmental variables explain Polynesian divergence: island climate (tropical to subantarctic), geology (atolls to continental fragments), marine resources (reef-rich to barren), area (100 acres to 103,000 sq miles), terrain fragmentation (smooth vs rugged valleys), isolation (complete to moderate). These variables determine societal complexity.

Key Concepts

Environmental determinism framework: six geographic variables. Climate determines agriculture viability. Geology determines available resources/tools. Marine resources limit subsistence options. Island area limits population size. Terrain affects political unification. Isolation affects inter-island contact/trade. All affect societal development.

Historical & Geographic Context

Six environmental factors vary dramatically across Polynesian islands. Climate range: tropical to subantarctic. Area range: 100 acres to 103,000 sq miles. Geology range: bare atolls to resource-rich continents. These variations determine different Polynesian societies.
