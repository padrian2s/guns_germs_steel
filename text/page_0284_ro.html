Rezumat

Irrigation system hypothesis: states formed through necessity to coordinate irrigation systems. However, evidence disproves: Mesoamerica and Andes irrigation systems remained small-scale, locally maintainable without central authority. States formed there for reasons other than irrigation necessity. Irrigation emerged as secondary consequence of formed states, not cause. Geographic pattern: irrigation-necessary states (Egypt, Mesopotamia, China) centralized, but irrigation-unnecessary states (Europe, Americas) also centralized. Irrigation not explaining state formation universally.
