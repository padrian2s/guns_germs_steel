Diamond traces the historical evolution of racist explanations: Darwinian "survival of the fittest" theory, then genetic frameworks claiming European genetic superiority over Africans and especially Aboriginal Australians. While modern Western society publicly rejects racism, many individuals privately maintain racist assumptions. Diamond argues that Australia presents a seemingly compelling racist argument: whites built civilization within a century on the same continent where Aborigines remained hunter-gatherers for 40,000 years.

Concepte Cheie

Diamond identifies the "Aboriginal Australian thought experiment" as a deceptive racist argument: identical environment + different peoples = different outcomes, seemingly proving racial differences. He counters that this reasoning ignores what allowed whites to succeed: imported technology, animals, and knowledge from Eurasia. The superficial logic masks deeper geographical causes.
