Europeans initially settled coast, penetration into interior slow. By 1960 Europeans reached interior. Pre-1526: 12,000+ years independent development with zero European contact. Key Concepts

European expansion temporally rapid but geographically slow into New Guinea interior. Terrain barriers (mountains, jungles) restricted European inland movement. Coastal penetration easy; interior penetration required 100+ years. European military superiority ineffective in terrain. Technology transfer slow due to geographic barriers preventing transport/communication. Distance and terrain more limiting than technological superiority.
