Summary

Americas domesticated animals limited: Mexico had only dogs, no pack animals, no edible livestock. Mexican turkeys/sunflowers would thrive in Andes but tropical climate barrier prevented southward spread. Corn, squash, beans: 700-mile distance separated Mexico from US Southwest but diffusion required thousands of years due to climate gradients. North-south geographic axis constrained crop diffusion speed.

Key Concepts

North-south crop diffusion constrained. Geographic distance secondary to climate compatibility. Crops adapted to source climate fail in different climate zones. Americas domesticates capable of thriving elsewhere blocked by intervening climates. Geographic axis orientation determines diffusion speed independent of distance shortness.

Historical & Geographic Context

Mexico domesticated maize, beans, squash, turkeys. Limited domesticated animals (dogs only). Tropical Mexico climate unsuited for northward temperate US or southward Andean diffusion. 700-mile US Southwest distance required millennia for adaptation. Andes would accept turkeys/sunflowers but tropical intervening climate prevented spread. Americas domesticate inventory limited by climate constraints on geographic diffusion.

Connection to Main Thesis

Americas north-south axis geography cascades into domesticate diffusion constraints. Mexico innovations failed to spread north or south across climate zones. Eurasian east-west axis enabled rapid domesticate spread across similar climates. Geographic axis orientation determines which domesticates accessible to which civilizations. Americas geography limited technology diffusion reach.
