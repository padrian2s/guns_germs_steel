Summary

Population replacement through conquest, expulsion, interbreeding, killing, epidemics. Niger-Congo language distribution reveals Bantu as engulfing population. Physical and linguistic evidence infers prehistoric engulfings but doesn't explain mechanisms. Critical questions: What advantages enabled Bantu expansion? Why displaced other populations? Geographic and technological determinism underlying population replacement.

Key Concepts

Population replacement requires technological advantage enabling expansion. Bantu advantages geography-rooted enabling expansion enabling displacement. Domestication access determines expansion capacity determines population outcome. Population replacement mechanisms (conquest, epidemic, genetic absorption) follow technology advantage enabling expansion.

Historical & Geographic Context

Bantu expansion ~2000 years ago from Cameroon/Nigeria homeland southward/eastward. Replaced or absorbed Khoisan hunters and Pygmy populations. Mechanisms: warfare (iron weapons advantage), epidemic disease, displacement, genetic intermixture. Linguistic/physical evidence traces replacement but not mechanisms.
