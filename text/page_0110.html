Summary

Why did agriculture rise? Five main contributing factors: (1) Decline in wild food availability - big game extinction ~11,000 years ago forced alternative subsistence. (2) Climate change enabled plant growth. (3) Population growth required increased food production. (4) Available domesticable wild species. (5) Social/cultural factors. Geographic factors determined factor importance.

Key Concepts

Five factors in agriculture rise: wild animal decline, climate change, population pressure, species availability, cultural readiness. Geographic variation determines which factors mattered where. Regions with species AND population pressure â†’ agriculture. Regions without species OR without pressure â†’ no agriculture despite other factors.

Historical & Geographic Context

Post-Ice Age (~13,000 BP): megafauna extinctions globally. Climate warming 11000-9000 BP. Population growth pressure increased 10000-8000 BP. Fertile Crescent: all five factors combined â†’ agriculture. Australia: no suitable domesticable species despite population pressure â†’ no agriculture despite hunter-gatherer success.

Connection to Main Thesis

Agricultural emergence requires geographic coincidence of multiple factors. Species availability essential - without suitable species, agriculture impossible regardless of population pressure. Geographic distribution of domesticable wild species determines agricultural feasibility. Regions with species transition to farming; regions without species remain hunter-gatherers despite identical pressure.
