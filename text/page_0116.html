Summary

Plant seed dispersal mechanisms: widespread geographic variation. Many seeds equipped with dispersal aids: fleshy fruits, wind dispersal, animal carriers. Wild plant survival dependent on seed dispersal. Domestication requires losing dispersal traits (seeds retained on plant). Genetic variation in dispersal mechanisms geographic. Regions with many dispersal-capable species provide more domestication raw material.

Key Concepts

Seed dispersal mechanism diversity geographic. Species with diverse dispersal strategies provide wider domestication options. Domestication selection pressures uniform: remove dispersal. Available genetic variation determines domestication ease. Geographic plant distribution determines genetic diversity available.
