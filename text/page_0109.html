Key Concepts

Agriculture adoption rate inverse to wild food abundance. Rich foraging regions resist agricultural transition longer. Technology diffusion speeds vary by local carrying capacity saturation. Geographic wild food productivity determines adoption timeline regardless of crop availability.

Historical & Geographic Context

Mediterranean Europe: diverse wild foods supported population. Gradual agriculture adoption over centuries. Atlantic Europe: productive shellfish/fish resources delayed agriculture adoption. Japan: marine resources abundance resisted continental agriculture adoption for thousands of years.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic wild food abundance determines agriculture adoption resistance. Regions geographically rich in wild resources maintain foraging longest. Geographic poverty in wild resources forces rapid agriculture adoption. Same domesticated crops diffused differently based on local geographic carrying capacity. Geography determines adoption speed regardless of technology availability.
