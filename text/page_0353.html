Summary

East Polynesian colonists underwent adaptive diversification in different environments within millennium. Chatham Islands: reverted hunting-gathering (cold climate unsuitable agriculture). Hawaii: built protostate with intensive food production (tropical climate suitable). Environmental variation determined technology adoption/abandonment. Same colonists produced different societies based on geographic environmental suitability. European arrival enabled temporary colonial dominance through technological advantage. Key Concepts

Geographic environment determines technology viability. Cold climate unsuitable agriculture enforces hunting-gathering reversion. Tropical climate suitable agriculture enables state development. Same population different environments produces different societies. Technological adoption geography-contingent. European advantage temporary against adapted indigenous populations. Germs and food producers reciprocal exchange benefits neither hemisphere exclusively.
