Rezumat

Phaistos Disk printing invention (1700 BCE Crete): earliest printing technology. China printing ~2,500 years later; medieval Europe printing 3,100 years later. Central question: Why wasn't precocious Cretan printing technology widely adopted? Why invented in Crete at that time vs. Mesopotamia, Mexico, or other writing centers? Why did ink and roller technology take millennia to develop after initial Cretan stamp printing?

Technology spread rates determine whether independent reinvention or diffusion dominates.

Context Istoric È™i Geografic

Cretan printing 1700 BCE: advanced administrative system (palace records) created printing need. Crete geographic isolation limited diffusion; Mediterranean trade routes insufficient for technology spread. China printing ~1200 BCE: independent invention driven by similar administrative complexity (imperial bureaucracy). Medieval Europe printing 1450 CE: Gutenberg's invention responded to European economic expansion, demand for books, paper availability from Islamic technology diffusion, and metal-working sophistication enabling movable type.

Geographic conditions determine both invention necessity and diffusion possibility. Crete's initial printing technology failed adoption due to limited practical advantage over hand-writing (Linear B script complex, limited economic incentive). Geographic diffusion barriers meant Mediterranean cultures couldn't copy Cretan methods. Independent reinvention in China and Europe followed geographic development of similar administrative complexity independently.
