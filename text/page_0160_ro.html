Rezumat

Only 14 large herbivorous mammal species globally domesticated: sheep, goat, cattle, pig, horse (Eurasia), reindeer, yak, water buffalo, bactrian camel, dromedary camel, llama, alpaca (Andes only), guinea pig, dog (ubiquitous). Geographic distribution extremely uneven. Americas: only llama/alpaca in mountains. Africa: only guinea fowl beyond Eurasian species. Geographic monopoly determined civilizational capacity.

Concepte Cheie

Animal domestication rarity. Only 14 large herbivores ever domesticated. Feralization resistance, size, temperament, diet, breeding patterns determine domesticability. Most large mammals refuse domestication despite thousands of years effort. Only species with specific behavioral traits domesticate. Geographic distribution of suitable species completely determines animal domestication capacity.
