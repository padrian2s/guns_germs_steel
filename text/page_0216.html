Summary

Writing enabled political conquest organization. Monarchs/merchants conveyed colonizing fleet commands via writing. Fleet navigation: maps and written sailing directions from previous expeditions. Written accounts of earlier expeditions motivated later exploration. Accounts described wealth/fertile lands awaiting conquerors. Previous experience documented enabling future conquest planning.

Key Concepts

Writing enables large-organization command structure. Writing enables knowledge transmission across time (experience documentation). Writing enables long-distance coordination (maps, written orders). Writing enables organizational memory. Literate civilizations enable larger political units. Writing literacy determines organizational scale capability.

Historical & Geographic Context

European colonization required writing: maps, charts, sailing directions. Columbus expedition: benefited from previous voyage documentation. Spanish conquest: used written orders across Atlantic. Americas indigenous societies: lacked writing in many regions. Aztecs/Incas had limited writing. Indigenous inability to document/coordinate across large distances (no writing technology) reduced organizational capacity versus European literate coordination.
