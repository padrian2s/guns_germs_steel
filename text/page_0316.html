Summary

Minimal New Guinea cultural diffusion to Australia despite proximity: intermarriage transferred physical traits (coiled hair) and language features (unusual phonemes in four Cape York languages) to Cape York Peninsula. Diffusion limited despite sustained contact because of geographic distance, intervening warfare, and small population transfers. New Guinea influence visible archaeologically but technologically insignificant. Intermarriage channels enabled human gene/trait flow but not technology transfer.

Key Concepts

Biological/cultural trait diffusion (hair types, phonemes) easier than technology diffusion. Physical traits transmit through reproduction; technologies require knowledge transmission. Language influence persists in isolated regions. Warfare/distance create diffusion barriers. Gene flow observable; technology adoption absent. Intermarriage creates cultural bridges but insufficient for technology transfer.
