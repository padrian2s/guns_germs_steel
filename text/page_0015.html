Summary

Diamond presents the prologue's central question asked by Yali, a New Guinean politician: why do Europeans possess more cargo (technology, wealth, goods) than New Guineans? This seemingly simple question masks a profound puzzle about global inequality that historians have largely abandoned.

Key Concepts

The core question concerns why disparities in wealth and power exist between populations. Diamond reformulates this into broader historical inquiry: why didn't Native Americans, Africans, and Aboriginal Australians dominate Europeans instead? Historical & Geographic Context

Diamond's personal encounter with Yali in 1972 New Guinea prompted this investigation. By the book's writing (1997), a century of colonial expansion had already reshaped global power dynamics. Diamond's timing challenges historical amnesia about why certain continents achieved technological superiority by the 15th-16th centuries.

Connection to Main Thesis

Yali's question becomes the framework for Diamond's geographical determinism thesis.
