Summary

Diamond examines challenges in dating pre-Clovis claims in Americas. The Pedro Furada site illustrates problems: cave paintings exist, but supposed "tools" at cliff base may be naturally broken rocks; charcoal dates ~35,000 years ago but links to paintings unclear. Archaeological caution necessaryâ€”published in Nature despite questionable evidence.

Key Concepts

Dating problems in archaeology. Charcoal contamination confusion. Difference between undisputed human artifacts (Clovis points, Cro-Magnon tools) vs ambiguous objects. Necessity of rigorous evidence standards in prehistoric claims.

Historical & Geographic Context

Pedro Furada (Brazil) ~35,000 years ago disputed; Meadowcroft (Pennsylvania) ~16,000 years ago questioned; Monte Verde (Chile) ~15,000 years ago more convincing. Pre-Clovis evidence remains archaeologically controversial.

Connection to Main Thesis

Pre-Clovis colonization debate affects timeline of Americas settlement. Earlier colonization = longer development time = different geographic advantage calculation. Geography's role in development depends critically on accurate settlement timing.
