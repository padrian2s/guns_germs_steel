Island megafauna extinction pattern: human arrival â†’ rapid bird extinction. Geographic variation: some islands lacked domesticated animals due to canoe transport failure or unsuitability. Tropical crops impossible at high latitudes. Geographic factors determine subsistence mix available.

Historical & Geographic Context

Polynesian colonization ~1200 BCE onward. Island-by-island variation in fauna availability. Cold Chathams/New Zealand South: agriculture impossible, revert to hunting. Tropical islands: agriculture viable with brought crops/animals.

Connection to Main Thesis

Food production base determined by geographic factors: available fauna, climate suitability for crops, transport capability across ocean. These geographic variables directly determined population density capacity, subsequently influencing social/political complexity possible on each island.
