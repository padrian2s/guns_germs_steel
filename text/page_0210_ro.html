Rezumat

Syphilis evolution: initially virulent (1495 Europe), became milder. Early syphilis: pustules head-to-knees, facial flesh loss, rapid death. Modern syphilis: genital focus, slow development, eventual death after years. Pathogen-host coevolution: mutation toward reduced virulence. Evolution favors transmission over mortality. Milder strains spread more than fatal variants.

Concepte Cheie

Pathogen evolution toward coexistence. Extreme virulence paradoxically limits spread (kills host before transmission). Mild virulence enables transmission chain continuation. Selection pressure toward reduced virulence over generations. Host-pathogen arms race. Virulence-transmission tradeoff determines evolutionary outcome.
