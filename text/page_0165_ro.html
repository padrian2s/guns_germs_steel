Rezumat

Pets vs. domestication distinction. Pet-taming universal across all traditional societies/continents. Pet animal variety far exceeds domesticated species. Tamed animals include unexpected species: kangaroos, possums, birds (flycatchers, ospreys). Pet ownership demonstrates human taming capability but not domestication success. Most pets are temporary, not breeding animals.

Concepte Cheie

Taming vs. domestication. Taming: individual animal behavioral change. Domestication: genetic population change enabling breeding. Humans can tame any species (with effort). Domestic animals require genetic traits enabling reproduction, docility, feeding, fertility. Pet-taming proves human flexibility; domestication rarity proves genetic constraints.
