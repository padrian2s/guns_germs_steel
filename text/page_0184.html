Summary

Plant growth determined by climate features. Tropical rainforest confined ~10 degrees latitude equator. Mediterranean scrub 30-40 degrees latitude. Plant germination, growth, disease resistance adapted to specific climate features. Day-length, temperature, rainfall trigger seed germination. Genetic plant traits match regional climate precisely. Geographic climate determines plant adaptation fundamentally.

Key Concepts

Plant genetics climate-adapted. Day-length signals: germination cues in northern latitudes. Temperature thresholds: growth season length determines crop viability. Rainfall patterns: germination triggers. Disease resistance: adapted to regional pathogens. Geographic climate variation produces geographic crop variation through genetic adaptation.

Historical & Geographic Context

Tropical crops: short-day triggers adapted to equatorial constant day-length. Temperate crops: long-day triggers adapted to seasonal variation. Mediterranean crops: summer-dry season adaptation. Geographic climate determine crop suitability. Plant distribution across latitudes reflects genetic climate adaptation. Moving crops north/south requires climate compatibility.

Connection to Main Thesis

Plant genetics geography-determined through climate adaptation. Geographic latitude determines climate features determining crop genetic requirements. Moving crops across climates fails due to incompatibility. Geographic diffusion success depends on climate/latitude matching. Plant genetics climate-specific: geographic movement limited by climatic boundaries.
