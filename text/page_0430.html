Summary

Further Readings bibliography: Burenhult's "Illustrated History of Humankind" five-volume series: The First Humans, People of the Stone Age, Old World Civilizations, New World/Pacific Civilizations, Traditional Peoples Today. Comprehensive human history from prehistory to contemporary. Geographic coverage across all regions. Source evidence for geographic determinism argument.

Key Concepts

Comparative human history evidence. First humans geographic origins and dispersal. Stone Age human adaptation to diverse geographies. Civilization emergence geography-specific. Regional civilization variation patterns. Contemporary peoples maintaining traditional geographic adaptations.

Historical & Geographic Context

Burenhult series: First Humans documents African origins and geographic dispersal. Stone Age volumes document geographic human adaptation diversity. Old World Civilizations shows geographic centers (Middle East, Egypt, Indus, China). New World/Pacific shows independent geographic development. Traditional Peoples shows geographic resource adaptation. Comprehensive geographic approach throughout series.

Connection to Main Thesis

Comprehensive bibliography substantiating geographic determinism across human history. Humans distributed geographically achieving different developments based on geography. Ancient humans adapted regionally through geographic pressure. Civilizations emerged geographically in multiple independent centers. Burenhult series provides evidence foundation: human history geography-determined from Africa origins through modern regional variation.
