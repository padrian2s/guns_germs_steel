Rezumat

Greater Australia (New Guinea + Australia): developed both animal husbandry and agriculture. But Australian hemisphere developed neither. During Ice Ages Australia supported even more large marsupials than currently. Aboriginal Australians lacked domesticable animals (unlike Eurasian megafauna). Animals went extinct ~50,000 years ago before domestication possibility. Geography determined which megafauna persisted enabling domestication.

Concepte Cheie

Megafauna extinction determines domestication possibility. Large animals available determine whether herding possible. Australia lost large marsupials; inability to domesticate wild remainders prevented pastoral economy. Geography determined which animals survived Ice Ages. Surviving animals determine economic possibilities.
