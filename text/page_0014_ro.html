Rezumat

Diamond describes his 1972 meeting with Yali, a New Guinean politician traveling to prepare his people for independence from Australian mandate. Despite high school education and no foreign travel, Yali impressed Diamond with his intelligence, curiosity, and penetrating questions about bird evolution, human migration, and colonization.

Concepte Cheie

Yali represents an intellectually capable non-Western individual whose limited access to technology does not reflect limited intelligence. Diamond establishes that the technological gap between New Guinea and Europe cannot be explained by cognitive differences. Yali's questions reveal engagement with complex evolutionary and historical concepts.

Set in 1972, when Papua New Guinea was still under Australian administration but approaching independence, Diamond's encounter occurred during a period of rapid decolonization. Yali embodied the aspirations of newly independent nations questioning why they lacked the "cargo" (technology, material wealth) of colonizers.
