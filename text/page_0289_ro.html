Political hierarchies consolidate progressively when conditions permit: tribes consolidate into chiefdoms, chiefdoms consolidate into states, states consolidate into empires. Each consolidation driven by military advantage of larger units. Large units potentially enjoy military advantages over small units (superior troop numbers, specialized soldiers, logistical organization)â€”provided large units internally solve coordination problems. Consolidation continues until geographic carrying capacity limits reached or consolidation costs exceed military-advantage benefits.

Concepte Cheie

Military selection pressure drives hierarchical consolidation. Larger militaries defeat smaller militaries when geography enables organization. Organized large armies overcome disorganized populaces. Political consolidation arms race: geography enabling one group to consolidate forces neighbors to consolidate competing larger units. Consolidation stabilizes at scale matching geography's carrying capacity. Geography enables: population density determines achievable unit size; trade routes enable communication enabling coordination; resources enable army support.
