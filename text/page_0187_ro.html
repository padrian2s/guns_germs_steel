Concepte Cheie

Geographic climatic barriers to agriculture: tropical crops require specific climate, cannot adapt to Mediterranean climates. Livestock diffusion possible across climates (through management); crop diffusion constrained by climate zones. Population technological differences result from geographic barriers to diffusion, not from biological/intellectual differences. Khoisan peoples equally capable of agriculture but geographic barriers prevented technology access.

Context Istoric È™i Geografic

Fertile Crescent agriculture: included both tropical crops (originating Sub-Saharan Africa) and temperate crops/livestock. Sub-Saharan Africa: developed tropical agriculture but limited large domesticable animals. South Africa Fish River: climate boundary separating tropical Sub-Saharan environment from Mediterranean temperate environment. Tropical crops cannot survive Mediterranean climate; temperate crops cannot survive tropical climate. Khoisan populations on Mediterranean side received livestock (adaptable) but not crops (climate-dependent).

Geographic climate boundaries determine technology diffusion success. Technology suited to geography spreads; technology unsuited to geography doesn't. Khoisan-Bantu population differences result from geography (climate barriers) not from population biological differences. Livestock acquired showed Khoisan capacity for technology adoption. Geographic determinism explains population technological variation without biological determinism.
