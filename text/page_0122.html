Summary

Plant reproductive barriers preventing easy domestication: self-incompatible hermaphrodites (cannot self-fertilize), dioecious species (separate male/female plants). Domestication requires genetic manipulation overcoming reproductive isolation. Ancient farmers needed mutant self-compatible varieties or orchards with nearby cross-compatible individuals. Reproductive barriers delayed domestication of apples, pears, plums, cherries until classical times.

Key Concepts

Reproductive biology as domestication constraint. Self-incompatibility barriers preventing easy seed saving. Dioecy requiring two plant types in same location. Genetic variation in reproduction mechanisms. Regions lacking self-compatible mutants cannot rapidly domesticate self-incompatible species. Reproductive barriers create geographic variation in domestication ease.
