Summary

Genetic evidence proves independent domestication. Indian zebu cattle independently domesticated from aurochs, separate from Western Eurasian cattle. Zebu/Western ancestors diverged hundreds of thousands of years pre-domestication. Both domesticated within last 10,000 years, independently, each region domesticating local aurochs populations.

Key Concepts

Independent domestication of same species in different regions. Genetic divergence in wild ancestors predates domestication. Multiple domestication events from diverged wild populations. Geographic separation of wild populations enables multiple independent domestication of same species type.

Historical & Geographic Context

India and Western Eurasia: separate aurochs populations for millennia. Indian domestication ~8000 BCE creates zebu breeds. Western Eurasia domestication ~8000 BCE creates humpless breeds. Geographic separation of wild aurochs populations enabled independent domestication trajectories.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic separation of wild animal populations enables independent domestication. Aurochs distributed across Eurasia. India's population domesticated independently. Western Eurasian population domesticated independently. Geographic distribution of wild ancestors determines domestication origin patterns. Same species, different geography, different domestication outcome.
