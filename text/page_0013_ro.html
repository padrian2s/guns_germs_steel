Rezumat

Diamond opens the Prologue by establishing that over 13,000 years since the Ice Age, different continents developed vastly different technologies: some achieved literate industrial societies with metal tools, others developed only farming societies, and still others remained hunter-gatherers with stone tools. These differences shaped the modern world fundamentally.

Concepte Cheie

Diamond identifies "history's broadest pattern": differential development rates across continents in 13,000 years. Three tiers of societies (literate/metal, farming, hunter-gatherer) reflect increasing technological sophistication. These technological differences were the proximate cause of modern global inequality through conquest and genocide.
