Rezumat

Three contrasting agricultural origins compared: Fertile Crescent (one extreme), New Guinea and eastern U.S. (opposite extreme). Fertile Crescent: earliest domestication, most species domesticated, most productive/valuable species. New Guinea and eastern U.S.: later domestication, fewer species, less productive species. Geographic variation in domesticable flora determines agricultural success gradient.

Concepte Cheie

Geographic variation in domesticable species. Fertile Crescent: wild grains (wheat, barley) large-seeded, productive. New Guinea: root crops less productive. Eastern U.S.: limited domesticable species. Different geographic flora produces different agricultural outcomes despite parallel cultural motivation and thousands-of-years timeframe.
