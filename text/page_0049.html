Summary

Why is pre-Clovis evidence hard to prove if people really arrived earlier? Hundreds of American sites date 2000-11,000 BCE with unquestioned human remains, yet deeper older layers at same sites contain only animal remainsâ€”no human evidence. Europe's pre-Clovis evidence overwhelmingly exceeds Americas. Australia/New Guinea has even fewer archaeologists but many more unequivocal pre-Clovis sites.

Key Concepts

Logical problem: if pre-Clovis settlement existed, evidence should be abundant by now. Long hidden populations at low density/poor archaeological visibility unprecedented elsewhere. Either Americas settled ~11,000 BCE quickly, or earlier but invisibly with little impact until 11,000 BCE. Either way: shortest human prehistory on New World continents.

Historical & Geographic Context

Clovis culture securely dated 11,000 BCE. Earlier claims disputed despite intense archaeological effort. Australia/New Guinea evidence overwhelming by contrast. Geographic determinism requires accurate settlement chronologies.

Connection to Main Thesis

Americas settlement timing establishes baseline for comparing continental development. Shortest occupation = least time accumulating technological advantages. Geographic isolation + late settlement = compounded disadvantage in civilization development versus Old World.
