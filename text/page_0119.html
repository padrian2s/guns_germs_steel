Summary

Selection criteria for wild plant domestication diverse: size, taste, fleshy fruit, seedless fruit, oily seeds, fiber length. Wild squashes/pumpkins: minimal flesh around seeds. Cultivated varieties: vastly more flesh. Cultivated bananas: seedless (reproductive isolation required). Wild berries: difficult harvest. Cultivated: concentrated ripening.

Key Concepts

Domestication selection pressures vary by crop and region. Size, taste, fleshy fruit, seedlessness, synchronous ripening, reduced toxins. Natural genetic variation supplies selection material. Regions with diverse wild crops selected for different favorable traits. Geographic crop diversity determines trait diversity achievable.

Historical & Geographic Context

Fertile Crescent: wheat/barley selected for grain size. Mesoamerica: maize selected for kernel size, squash selected for flesh. Old World: grape selected for flavor and seedlessness. Each region adjusted selection to available wild species variation and local preferences.

Connection to Main Thesis

Available wild species genetic diversity determines domestication diversity. Regions with many domesticable plants achieve diverse crops with varied traits. Regions with few domesticable plants limited to narrow trait range. Geographic crop diversity geographically determined by wild species availability.
