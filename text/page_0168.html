Summary

Modern animal domestication experiments: elk (red deer) farms Scotland, moose farms Russia, bison experiments Ukraine, Kenya, Zimbabwe, South Africa. Modern attempts achieved only limited success. Bison meat occasionally appears in supermarkets; moose rarely ridden/worked. Modern technology cannot domesticate animals that ancient peoples couldn't. Demonstrates animal domestication biology-constrained, not culturally-optional.

Key Concepts

Animal domestication limits biologically-determined. Modern technology unable to overcome behavioral/physiological constraints. Temperament, breeding patterns, diet, growth rate genetic constraints. Geographic fauna distribution determines domestication feasibility. Geographic limitations absolute: modern technology confirms ancient constraints real and insurmountable.

Historical & Geographic Context

Modern experiments: Rowett Research Institute elk farming; Russian moose programs; African game farm attempts. Limited success despite modern infrastructure, veterinary knowledge, selective breeding technology. Moose unsuitable for riding despite efforts. Bison retained wild temperament. Confirms ancient peoples exploited only truly domesticable species.

Connection to Main Thesis

Animal domestication geography-determined through species biology. Geographic fauna distribution absolute constraint. Modern technology cannot overcome biological constraints on domestication. Geographic species distribution determines agricultural technology feasibility independent of cultural effort. Animals unavailable to ancient peoples remain unavailable to moderns. Geography determines domestication opportunities absolutely.
