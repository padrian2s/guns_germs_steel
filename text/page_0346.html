Summary

Bismarck/Solomon islanders physically intermediate between highlanders (Wiwor-like) and Indonesians (Achmad-like), though more similar to original populations. Sauakari example: wavy hair intermediate between coiled and straight, skin paler than original but darker than Indonesian. Genetic analysis confirms mixing. Physical evidence documents historical population admixture. Population genetics reflect mixed ancestry from ancient New Guineans and Austronesian invaders.

Key Concepts

Physical traits reflect population ancestry proportions. Intermediate characteristics indicate mixed ancestry. Genetic evidence corroborates historical population movements. Island populations show mixed genetics more than mainland populations. Population admixture documents contact intensity and duration. Genetic gradients reveal expansion and replacement patterns.

Historical & Geographic Context

Bismarck/Solomon islands: Austronesian settlement 3,600 years ago among original New Guinean populations. Extended contact and intermarriage resulted in genetic mixing. Modern populations show characteristics of both parent populations. Geographic island setting created contact conditions enabling gradual admixture over millennia.
