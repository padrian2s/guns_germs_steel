Rezumat

African crop origins geography-determined by climate zones: different regions developed domesticated crops suited to local climate conditions. Saharan climate: hot/dry prevents temperate crop growth. Sahel zone: summer rainfall (not winter like Fertile Crescent). Climate incompatibility prevents Fertile Crescent crop adoption: winter-rain crops cannot survive summer-rain climate. Geographic climate barriers determine agricultural composition by region.

Concepte Cheie

Crop adaptation climate-specific: winter-rain crops cannot grow in summer-rain climate. Geographic climate zones determine domesticated species: separate origins for different climate regions. Crop diffusion requires climate compatibility: temperate crops don't spread into tropical zones, tropical crops don't spread into temperate zones. Climate barriers as absolute as water barriers: prevent both crop spread and animal spread.

Sahel zone: rainforest transition zone requiring specific climate-adapted crops. Each African region: independent crop domestication reflecting climate suitability.
