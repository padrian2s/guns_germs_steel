Summary

Norse colonization of Americas succeeded in Arctic/sub-Arctic (Iceland A.D. 874, Greenland A.D. 986, North American visits 1000-1350), but ultimately failed. Geographic constraints and population source limitations prevented sustained colonization. Norse attempt demonstrates geography-determined colonization successâ€”latitude, climate, source population wealth determine viability.

Key Concepts

Colonization success geography-dependent: source population (wealth, size), target climate (food productivity), latitude (agriculture suitability), supporting homeland resources. Arctic/sub-Arctic unsuitable food production limits expansion. Source population limited (Norway) constrains colonization scale. Geographic isolation prevents sustained colonization. Population replacement requires sufficient settler population and productive agriculture.
