Summary

Chapter 5: "History's Haves and Have-Nots" frames the fundamental pattern: societies with farmer power versus without it. Food production never arose in large globe areas for ecological reasons. Ecological constraints that prevented agriculture in prehistory persist today. Agricultural absence correlates with developmental inequality.

Key Concepts

Geographic inequality and agriculture. Societies adopting agriculture early acquired power advantages permanently. Geographic regions where agriculture impossible remained hunter-gatherer powered, militarily/organizationally disadvantaged in contact with agricultural societies. Path dependency and geographic constraints compound over millennia.

Historical & Geographic Context

Agricultural development unequal globally. Fertile Crescent ~10000 BCE. Sub-Saharan Africa, Australia, Arctic, Pacific islands: agriculture never arose, remained hunter-gatherer societies. Chapter 5 frames this as central to understanding inequality between peoples at moment of European contact (15th-16th centuries).
