Summary

American megafauna extinctions occurred ~11,100 BCE (within century or two), with Shasta ground sloth and Harrington's mountain goat dated with exceptional precision in Grand Canyon area. This extinction timing precisely matches Clovis arrival (~11,000 BCE), supporting the overkill hypothesis that human hunting caused megafauna collapse across the Americas.

Key Concepts

Historical & Geographic Context

~11,100 BCE megafauna extinction in Americas. Clovis sites appearing just before 11,000 BCE. Extinction timing (century-precise dating) matches colonization timing. Sloth and goat extinctions well-documented in Grand Canyon archaeological record.

Connection to Main Thesis

Americas megafauna extinction removed potential domestication animals (unlike Eurasia's horses, cattle). Geographic difference in megafauna survival/extinction created domestication disparity. Late colonization + megafauna extinction = no livestock base, limiting agricultural surplus and technological development compared to Eurasia.
