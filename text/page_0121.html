Summary

Wild plants evolved germination inhibitors: dormancy mechanisms spreading seed germination across multiple years. Risk reduction strategy: simultaneous germination risks extinction from single bad season. Dormancy ensures some seeds survive unfavorable conditions. Domestication selected against dormancy preference (farmers want immediate harvest-ready germination).

Key Concepts

Wild plant evolutionary strategy: germination dormancy and timing heterogeneity. Genetic variation in dormancy timing available to farmers. Artificial selection for rapid germination opposing wild strategy. Unconscious selection through harvesting immediately-germinating plants (harvesters avoid dormant seeds). Domestication selection pressure: germination speed.

Historical & Geographic Context

Wild wheat naturally staggered germination across seasons. Fertile Crescent farmers unconsciously selected non-dormant varieties through immediate harvesting. Mesoamerica: teosinte similarly showed dormancy inhibited by farmer selection. Domestication reduced dormancy period significantly across regions.
