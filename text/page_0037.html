Key Concepts

Eurasia's continuous landmass allowed human spread, but water barriers (Australia, Americas) prevented colonization until technological advancement. This geographical isolation meant some continents developed civilizations for longer periods before external contactâ€”a potential advantage or disadvantage depending on circumstances.

Historical & Geographic Context

Europe's human occupation began ~500,000 years ago; Australia and Americas remained isolated until ~15,000-50,000 years ago. Geographic barriers directly determined when different continents' human development began relative to each other.

Connection to Main Thesis

Geographic barriers that prevented human colonization of Australia/Americas gave Eurasia/Africa potential head start, but this depended on whether early occupation actually generated technological advantages. Geography structured opportunity but didn't determine outcomes automatically.
